# Week 10 — Challenges

Two challenges, ~90 minutes each. Where the exercises had you build one specific model and check it against a known answer, these ask you to **change an assumption and reason about the direction and size of the effect before you compute it** — the actual skill a deal team gets paid for is not running the model once, it's knowing which lever to pull and defending what happens when you pull it.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Stock vs. cash tradeoff](challenge-01-stock-vs-cash-tradeoff.md)** — hold the purchase price fixed and swing the financing mix from 100% cash to 100% stock; compare the results on EPS, leverage, and ownership dilution, and recommend a mix.
2. **[Challenge 2 — LBO return drivers](challenge-02-lbo-return-drivers.md)** — decompose the LBO's 1.95x MOIC into the three separate levers that produced it, then stress-test each one independently.

## Before you start

- Both exercises for this challenge set (Exercise 1 for Challenge 1; Exercise 3 for Challenge 2) are complete and match their "Expected results" tables.
- You're comfortable re-running a model with one input changed and comparing the new output to the old one — that's the entire mechanic both challenges ask for.

## How these differ from the exercises

An exercise tells you exactly what to compute and gives you a number to check against. A challenge gives you a *question* — "which financing mix should the board choose, and why" or "which lever drove this return" — and expects you to decide what to compute, in what order, to answer it defensibly. There is more than one reasonable way to organize the analysis in each challenge; there is only one right final number for the underlying arithmetic itself, which is why "Expected results" ranges are provided even though the write-up around them is yours to construct.
