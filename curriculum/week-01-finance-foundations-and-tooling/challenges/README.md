# Week 1 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, these have **no single right answer** — the skill is building something real and defending a position, not recall. Do them after the three exercises.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Amortization schedule engine](challenge-01-amortization-schedule-engine.md)** — build a full loan amortization schedule, stored in SQL and generated with Python, for any principal/rate/term combination. *(~90 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — Spreadsheet vs. pipeline writeup](challenge-02-spreadsheet-vs-pipeline-writeup.md)** — take a real, specific financial workflow and argue, with evidence, why it belongs in a SQL + Python pipeline instead of a workbook. *(~90 min.)*

## How these are judged

There's no answer key with exact numbers to match. Instead, each challenge tells you what a *strong* submission looks like. You're being graded on:

- **Correctness of the core mechanics** — does the amortization math actually balance? Does the writeup's technical claims hold up?
- **Reusability** — could someone else point your engine at a different loan, or your argument at a different workflow, and have it still work?
- **Explicit reasoning** — do you show your work and justify the choices you made, not just present a finished artifact?

Keep your work in `challenge-01/` and `challenge-02.md` with your code **and** your written reasoning. The reasoning is the point.
