# Week 6 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, these have **no single right answer** — the skill is building something real and defending a position under pressure, not recall. Do them after the three exercises.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Covenant breach simulation](challenge-01-covenant-breach-simulation.md)** — model a recession scenario against Crunch Machine Co.'s term loan and find exactly where its covenants break. *(~90 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — Mixed financing optimization](challenge-02-mixed-financing-optimization.md)** — stop treating equity and debt as all-or-nothing, and find the mix that raises the $20,000,000 at the lowest true cost while staying within covenant headroom. *(~90 min.)*

## How these are judged

There's no answer key with exact numbers to match. Instead, each challenge tells you what a *strong* submission looks like. You're being graded on:

- **Correctness of the core mechanics** — do your covenant tests and cost calculations actually compute what they claim to?
- **Reusability** — could someone point your code at a different EBITDA scenario, or a different equity/debt split, and have it still work?
- **Explicit reasoning** — do you show your work and justify the choices you made, not just present a finished number?

Keep your work in `challenge-01/` and `challenge-02/` with your code **and** your written reasoning. The reasoning is the point.
