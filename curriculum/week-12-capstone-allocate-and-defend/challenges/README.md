# Week 12 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Like every challenge in this course, these have **no single right answer** — the skill being graded is judgment under scrutiny, not recall. Do them after the three exercises, once you have a valuation, a risk read, and a memo draft to actually attack.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Red-team your own thesis](challenge-01-red-team-the-thesis.md)** — attack the weakest assumptions in your own base case with numbers, not vibes. *(~90 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — Diagnose a governance failure](challenge-02-governance-failure-case.md)** — take one `capstone_governance_cases` row, reconstruct the incentive logic that produced it, and design the fix. *(~90 min.)*

## How these are judged

There's no answer key with a single correct number. Each challenge tells you what a *strong* submission looks like. You're being graded on:

- **Rigor of the attack** — did you find the assumption that actually matters most, quantified, not just the one that's easiest to complain about?
- **Honesty about your own work** — a red team that only finds small problems in its own thesis usually isn't looking hard enough.
- **Specificity of the fix** — "better governance" is not an answer; "a lead independent director with authority over related-party approvals" is.

Keep your work in `challenge-01.md` / `challenge-02.md` with your queries/scripts **and** your written reasoning. As with every challenge this course, the reasoning is the point — and this week, so is your willingness to find the hole in your own case before someone else does.
