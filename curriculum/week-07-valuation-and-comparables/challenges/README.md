# Week 7 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, these have **no single right answer** — the skill is building something real and defending a position, not recall. Do them after the three exercises.

1. **[Challenge 1 — DCF sensitivity & football field](challenge-01-dcf-sensitivity-football-field.md)** — build a WACC × terminal-growth sensitivity grid for the DCF, then assemble every method this week produced into one football-field chart. *(~90 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — Reconcile DCF vs. comps](challenge-02-reconcile-dcf-vs-comps.md)** — quantify, with real numbers, exactly why the DCF and the comps analysis disagree, and defend a final recommended range. *(~90 min.)*

## How these are judged

There's no answer key with exact numbers to match. Instead, each challenge tells you what a *strong* submission looks like. You're being graded on:

- **Correctness of the core mechanics** — does the sensitivity grid actually respond correctly to each input? Does the reconciliation's math hold up under a second look?
- **Reusability** — could someone else point your sensitivity engine at different assumptions, or your reconciliation logic at a different valuation, and have it still work?
- **Explicit reasoning** — do you show your work and justify the choices you made (which comps to trust, how to weight disagreeing methods), not just present a finished number?

Keep your work in `challenge-01/` and `challenge-02/` with your code **and** your written reasoning. The reasoning is the point — a valuation number with no defensible argument behind it is not a finished valuation.
